# Makar District Health Office (MDHO) Disaster Pharmacy Guidelines

This document outlines the standard operating procedures (SOPs) for emergency medication management during flood disasters in the Makar District of Pahang.

---

## 1. Safety Buffer Stock Multiplier
* **Rule**: To prevent sudden stockouts due to road closures and supply chains being cut off, all frontline hospitals must maintain an emergency Safety Stock buffer multiplier.
* **Calculation**: The minimum required safety stock is **1.3 times (1.3x)** the projected daily expected doses (p50 expected surge) for the forecast horizon.
* **Procurement Trigger**: If local stock is below this 1.3x safety threshold, a deficit is declared and emergency stock replenishment must be initiated.

---

## 2. Intra-District Stock Relocation (Lending/Borrowing)
* **Rule**: Before requesting emergency supply orders from the main administrative hub, facilities must attempt to balance resources locally within the district.
* **Helper Facility Transfer Limit**: Excess stock can be relocated from Hospital Kampung Pisang (HKP) to Hospital Kuala Makar (HKM), or vice versa (lending from HKM to HKP).
* **Helper Safety Threshold**: In any stock transfer, the helping facility must never have its local capacity drop below **50%** of its baseline requirements. Stock relocation is capped at the helper's excess capacity limit.

---

## 3. Emergency Procurement from Makar District Health Office (MDHO)
* **Rule**: If the combined local stock and maximum available relocated stock still fail to meet the required safety buffer, an emergency order must be sent to the Makar District Health Office (MDHO).
* **MDHO Delivery Window**: Emergency orders sent to MDHO are guaranteed to be packaged, shipped, and delivered to the requesting facility within **48 hours (2 days)**.
* **Audit Trail**: Any emergency procurement order placed with MDHO requires the digital sign-off signature of the Disaster Pharmacy Manager.
